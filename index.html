What We Build

1. Structure-Response Modeling

Transforming physical response curves into structured state representations.

State variables are derived from experimentally observable dynamics.

  • Polymer structure-property mapping
  • Frequency-domain response modeling
  • Dynamic mechanical and dielectric systems
  • Material state estimation under defined conditions

2. Physics-Constrained Learning Systems

Developing models that:

  • Respect physical laws
  • Quantify uncertainty
  • Generalize across material families
  • Remain interpretable at feature level

Prediction is treated as constrained physical state estimation, not purely statistical regression.

Case Example

DMA-Based Structure-Property Intelligence Engine

A prototype modeling system that predicts storage modulus at 25C from:

  • Dynamic mechanical response (E'(T), tanδ(T))
  • Molecular and microstructural descriptors

Capabilities:

  • Curve parameterization
  • Log-stable predictive modeling
  • Group-aware validation
  • Confidence interval estimation
  • Physical consistency verification

Validation Strategy:
Group-aware cross-validation across material families.

This case demonstrates the feasibility of physics-informed state modeling in real material systems.
